Hi, I'm Marshall!

Thanks for visiting. Here's a little bit about me:

I am a self-taught Frontend Engineer focused on JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. Prior to learning software development, I was a civil engineer where I learned how to problem solve effectively and developed a passion for learning. My current studies are driven by my mission to shape the future of technology to benefit people from all backgrounds and my curiosity for how and why technology works.

Fun Facts: I have a love for travel photography, and I ran my first (and only) marathon in 2020.



My full story:

I'm a Midwest boy born and raised in Minnesota who loved math and science growing up, so it was only natural that I wanted to be an engineer. After high school I continued my formal education at community college where I won the award for the top mathematics student of the year. From there, I transferred to Auburn University for civil engineering where I led multiple student organizations, participated in research and teaching, and completed several internships. I graduated in May 2021 and planned to attend Stanford University for a master's in structural engineering, however, I had second thoughts about my career path during my internship the following summer. I chose to dip my toes into the world of computer science by taking Harvard's CS50 and absolutely loved it, so I decided to start teaching myself front-end web development full time. When I'm not coding, you can find me hiking a trail, snapping photos, or taste-testing IPAs at a local brewery.
